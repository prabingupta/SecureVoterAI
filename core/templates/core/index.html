About the Platform

Democracy Secured by Artificial Intelligence

SecureVoter AI was built for Islington College, Kathmandu — replacing paper ballots with a system where every voter's identity is verified by a 1404‑dimensional MediaPipe face embedding before a single vote is cast.

From multi‑stage liveness detection that blocks photo‑replay attacks, to AES‑256 encrypted ballot storage that makes individual choices mathematically unreadable — every layer of the system exists to protect both the integrity of elections and the privacy of voters.

Election Process

How SecureVoter AI Works

A six‑step verified pipeline — from account creation to encrypted result publication — engineered so that every vote is provably genuine and provably anonymous.

01
Account Registration

Create Your Voter Account

Submit your student ID, full name, department, and contact details. Your information is verified against the college registry before proceeding to biometric enrolment.

  • Student ID verification
  • Department confirmation
  • Secure password setup
02
Biometric Enrolment

Face Registration & Liveness Verification

MediaPipe FaceMesh captures 468 facial landmarks and generates a 1404‑dimensional embedding. Three consecutive liveness challenges confirm you are physically present.

  • 1404‑D landmark embedding
  • Anti‑spoof DFT + texture checks
  • Multi‑face guard (single person only)
03
Admin Approval

Account Reviewed & Approved

The Election Committee reviews your registration. Only verified Islington College students receive approval. Rejected accounts are notified with a reason.

  • Manual admin review layer
  • Fraud alert integration
  • Approval notification sent
04
Election Day Login

Two‑Factor Biometric Sign‑In

On voting day, log in with your password then pass a real‑time face‑verification challenge. Cosine similarity must reach ≥ 0.97.

  • Password + face = 2FA
  • Session expires in 5 minutes
  • 3‑strike lockout policy
05
Cast Your Vote

Select, Confirm & Submit

Select your candidate. A confirmation modal displays your choice — once confirmed, the vote is AES‑256 encrypted and stored. No reversal is possible.

  • Pre‑vote liveness re‑verification
  • Double‑confirmation modal
  • Encrypted immediately on submit
06
Results Publication

Transparent, Auditable Results

Once polls close, the Election Committee publishes aggregate results. Individual choices remain anonymous. A PDF audit report is generated automatically.

  • Aggregate‑only decryption
  • Bar chart breakdown per candidate
  • Tie‑detection with runoff support

Security Architecture

Eight Layers of Protection

Every vote passes through a chain of independent security controls — so the failure of any single layer cannot compromise the election.

01

MediaPipe Face Embedding

468‑landmark mesh produces a 1404‑D unit vector unique to each voter. Cosine similarity ≥ 0.97 required for identity confirmation.

Biometrics
02

Multi‑Stage Liveness Detection

Blink (EAR threshold), head‑turn (nose offset ratio), and DFT pixel‑grid analysis run in sequence to block any non‑live presentation.

Anti‑Spoof
03

AES‑256 Ballot Encryption

Each vote is encrypted the moment it is cast using AES‑256. Decryption keys are held only by authorised system administrators post‑election.

Cryptography
04

Multi‑Face Guard

Up to four simultaneous faces are detected. If more than one person is present in frame, the verification is immediately rejected.

Access Control
05

Real‑Time Fraud Alerts

Any cosine similarity below 0.80 triggers an automatic FraudAlert log entry with IP, timestamp, and similarity score for admin review.

Monitoring
06

Session Lockout Policy

Face‑verify sessions expire in 5 minutes. Three consecutive failed attempts trigger a 15‑minute account lockout with admin notification.

Rate Limiting
07

One Vote, One Voter

Database‑level unique constraint on (voter, election) pair enforces the single‑vote rule absolutely — no second submission is physically possible.

Integrity
08

Anonymous Ballot Storage

Encrypted vote data is decoupled from voter identity at the database layer. Results are published as aggregate totals only.

Voter Eligibility

Who Can Vote?

Participation is open to all currently enrolled students — subject to identity verification and Election Committee approval.

Eligibility Criteria
  • Currently enrolled Islington College student
  • Valid Student ID on file with registry
  • Completed biometric face enrolment
  • Account approved by Election Committee
  • Device with camera access (laptop/phone)
  • Must vote within the election window
Prohibited Actions
  • Attempting to register with another student's identity
  • Using photographs or screens during face verification
  • Having multiple people in frame during liveness check
  • Attempting to vote more than once
  • Tampering with or inspecting encrypted vote data
  • Sharing login credentials with any other person

Confidentiality Guarantee

Your vote is yours alone. AES‑256 encryption ensures that no individual ballot can ever be linked back to you by any person or system, including platform administrators. Results are published as aggregate totals only.
